consequencec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: https://w3id.org/AIRO#Consequence

The outcome of an action that has occured in an event by an agent. The consequences can be categorized based on temporal factors (short-term, long-term) and also on the basis of utility (risks, opportunities). These together can give a decision maker an understanding of the action and whether it is morally right, wrong, or grey. (Consequence IRI from: https://lov.linkeddata.es/dataset/lov/vocabs/airo)

duration of consequencec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.kracr.iiitd.edu.in/ontology/appl-e/DurationOfConsequence

The duration of a consequence could be either short-term or long-term and this aspect plays a role in the morality of an action. From a consequentialist viewpoint, if an action leads to a bad consequence as well as a good consequence, a temporal assessment can give insight into whether it is morally right or wrong. If the action leads to a bad short-term consequence but a good long-term consequence then it may be an overall morally right action.
has super-classes
characteristic of consequencec
has scoredp min 1
has members
long term consequenceni, short term consequenceni

moral intentionc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.kracr.iiitd.edu.in/ontology/appl-e/MoralIntention

Moral Intention is defined as the ability to prioritise moral values over other values and hence requires that the desired outcome is bound by moral values. (From A. C. Ping, Strategic Ethical Consultancy). Moral intent is the desire to act ethically when facing a decision and overcome the rationalization to not be ethical this time. Even if a person sees the ethical aspects of a decision and has the philosophical tools to make the right choice, he or she still needs to want to do the right thing. If a person has no moral intent, then moral awareness and moral decision making are useless. (From BEING YOUR BEST SELF, PART 3: MORAL INTENT, Ethics Unwrapped.)
is in range of
has moral intentionop
has members
bad moral intentionni, good moral intentionni, no moral intentionni

utility of consequencec back to ToC or Class ToC

The utility of consequence describes the affect of the action from a good and bad perspective. It is also important to note that some consequences are neither good nor bad, they are neutral. A stricter view states that all actions have some good and/or bad consequences. In an action where no consequence has occured, the action itself was futile, due to which it was a waste of energy for the agent, thus a bad consequence.
